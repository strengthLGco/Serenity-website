Mechanism of Action

GHK-Cu is a naturally occurring copper complex of the tripeptide glycyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine. Found in human plasma, saliva, and urine, it declines significantly with age — dropping from ~200 ng/mL at age 20 to ~80 ng/mL by age 60. Research shows it activates over 4,000 genes, switching on regenerative pathways and silencing destructive ones while stimulating collagen, elastin, and potent antioxidant defense.
