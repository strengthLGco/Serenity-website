### DAY 8 — Wednesday Apr 23
**Pillar:** Education & Myth-Busting
**Topic:** Reconstitution 101 — How Researchers Prepare Lyophilized Peptides
**Compound:** Bacteriostatic Water + General

**TikTok/Instagram Reel (60s):**
Hook: "If you're reconstituting peptides wrong, your entire experiment is compromised."

Script:
"Lyophilized peptides are freeze-dried into a stable powder. To use them in research, they must be reconstituted with bacteriostatic water — sterile water containing 0.9% benzyl alcohol as a preservative.

Step 1: Let both the peptide vial and BAC water reach room temperature. Cold mixing can cause aggregation.

Step 2: Draw the appropriate volume of BAC water. For a 5mg peptide vial, 2mL gives you 2.5mg/mL concentration. Simple math.

Step 3: Inject the BAC water slowly against the glass wall of the vial. Never spray directly onto the powder — it can denature the peptide.

Step 4: Gently swirl. Never shake. Shaking creates foam and can break peptide bonds.

Step 5: Store at 2-8 degrees Celsius. Reconstituted peptides are stable for approximately 30 days refrigerated.

This is basic lab technique that half the internet gets wrong.

For research purposes only."
